Tiarra Carey serves as the Unit Commander of the Potomac River Young Marines, a youth leadership and development program based in Washington, D.C. Under her leadership, the unit has grown into a premier example of the Young Marines’ national mission: Strengthening the lives of America's youth through teamwork, leadership, and discipline.

With a strong commitment to youth empowerment, Commander Carey has expanded recruitment, increased community partnerships, and positioned the Potomac River Young Marines as a trusted resource for youth leadership training, community service, and drug demand reduction initiatives. She has spearheaded impactful projects including high-visibility Color Guard services, regional encampments, and community outreach campaigns that engage youth ages 8–18 in meaningful, character-building activities.

Commander Carey’s leadership emphasizes service, accountability, and innovation. She is known for fostering an environment where young people thrive academically, personally, and physically while building the confidence and character needed to become the leaders of tomorrow.

Her dedication extends beyond youth development to include community engagement and strategic fundraising, ensuring the Potomac River Young Marines remains a vital program for families, schools, and civic organizations throughout the D.C. metropolitan area.
